Nelson Mandela

The man who who freed South Africa from apartheid

Nelson Mandela in his cell prison (revisit), 1994

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela life:

  • 1918 - Born in the Transkei, South Africa.
  • 1939 - Enters university.
  • 1940 - Expelled from Fort Hare university for activism.
  • 1942 - Begins to attend ANC meeting informally.
  • 1944 - Co-founds ANC Youth League.
  • 1948 - Elected national secretary of ANC Youth League, later to president
  • 1952 - Campaign of civil disobedience. Opens South Africa's fist black law practice with Oliver Tambo.
  • 1956 - One of 156 activists charged with high treason.
  • 1961 - Goes underground to form armed wing
  • 1964 - Sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage and high treason. Sento to the maximum-security jail on Robben Island.
  • 1990 - Released.
  • 1993 - Wins the Nobel PEace Prize with F.W. de Kierk, the last apartheid president.
  • 1994 - Elected president after the ANC wins South Africa's first multi-racial elections.
  • 1999 - Steps down after one term as president.
  • 2013 - Dies aged 95.

"I learned that courage was not the absence of fear, but the triumph over it. The brave man is not he who does not feel afraid, but he who conquers that fear."

Nelson Mandela
